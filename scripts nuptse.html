EXT. LONDON SCHOOLYARD - DAY
The scene unfolds against the vibrant and gritty backdrop of London, brick houses lining the streets. Our BOY strolls down the lane, different – no Nuptse, no swagger. Just an invitation for aggro. As he approaches the schoolyard, he catches the attention of a group of student outside the schoolyard, each sporting a Nuptse like an armor. Their HEAD HONCHO, spotting our BOY, narrows his eyes in scrutiny.

HEAD HONCHO

Oi, mate. You lost? Dressed like that, you're a right walking target, fam. Where's your protection?

BOY WITHOUT JACKET

Protection against the cold? It's just a bit of chill, innit?

HEAD HONCHO

No, fam! It ain't about the frost; it's the wolves you gotta watch, the bullies! You’re wearing an invitation to an ass-kicking going to school looking like that.

The HEAD HONCHO pauses, taking a deep breath as he surveys the schoolyard, he rubs the back of his neck, a gesture of annoyance mixed with a sense of duty, then locks eyes with our boy.

HEAD HONCHO (CONT'D)

Ah man, I don't know why I'm doing this, but imma help you out, bro. At this exact moment, you’re a bully’s wet dream. Imagine if a bully sees you without a Nuptse...

CUT TO:
A dynamic, Guy Ritchie-style montage ensues, the camera whipping between subjects with a Guy Ritchie energy. Quick cuts and slick transitions parade the Nuptse jacket in all its glory, we're shown the might of the Nuptse celebrating the diversity in style possible with a nuptse.

HEAD HONCHO (V.O. OUT OF FRAME)

Guy with Nuptse Jacket, guy with Nuptse Jacket, guy without, guy with Nuptse...

As the montage concludes, we return to the group, the BOY is now contemplative.

HEAD HONCHO (CONT'D)

Now fam, who do you think he’s going to bully?

BOY

The guy with glasses? Or I mean... Mountain-climber-guy. That seems like a weird identity, worthy of bullying, no?

HEAD HONCHO

Let me provide you insight into the mindset of a bully, bro; a bloke with glasses will always have glasses. But you, on the other hand, might not wear that outfit again. That's what bullies live for. They're artists in their own twisted way. And mountain-guy is untouchable, that’s a vintage Nuptse, bro.

The BOY, now aware of the stakes, feels the weight of his vulnerability. Sensing his nervousness the Honcho responds with a hint satisfaction in his voice

HEAD HONCHO

Don’t worry, fam. I got your back.

In a gesture of solidarity, the HEAD HONCHO throws the BOY a Nuptse jacket in its iconic pocketed form. Now united by Nuptsies the group enters the schoolyard, ready to face whatever comes their way.
